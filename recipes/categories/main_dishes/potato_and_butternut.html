Potato and Butternut Squash Gratin

Crispy shallots too!

Ingredients

  • 3 shallots
  • 2 cups of neutral oil
  • 1 1/2 pounds butternut squash
  • 3 medium sized red potatoes
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 4 mashed garlic cloves
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 ounces of grated gruyere cheese
  • 1 tbspn of salt
  • Ground pepper
  • Enough butter to coat the pan

Instructions

Before prepping the ingredients, preheat the oven to 400°F (205°C). Now mix the milk and heavy cream together in saucepan and set over medium heat. Add in your garlic, herbs, and season to taste while stirring. Once it starts to boil, turn the heat down low and simmer for 2-3 minutes. While it is simmering, slice the squash and potatoes into 1/8th inch slices and simmer it for 7 minutes. After 7 minutes, remove the potatoes and squash and let it cool separately.
Grease a baking pan with butter and alternate between layers of squash and potatoes. Between each layer, drizzle on about 2 tbspn of the cream/milk and 1oz of the cheese. Once all the potatoes and squash are used up, pour the remaining milk/cream and cheese on top. Bake in the oven for 20 to 25 minutes or until the cheese is golden brown. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.


While the gratin is baking, oil a separate saucepan and place on medium heat. Cut the shallots into thin rings, similar to onion rings and separate them. Add them to the saucepan and cook for 3-5 minutes or until they turn golden brown. Stir occaisonally so it doesn't stick together. Remove shallots from the oil and pat dry and season with salt. Add shallots ontop of the gratin when it's done baking.
